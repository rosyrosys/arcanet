All three samples come from a single invocation of python arcanet.py with seed 2026. The MIDI files are byte-identical across runs. The audio previews below were rendered from those MIDI files with a minimal additive-synthesis piano voice — pleasant enough for a preview, but nowhere near the rendering you would get from a real piano soundfont in a DAW.

Sample 1 — AABA_16 16 bars · 52 note events

A standard pop-song period: two A-phrase statements, a contrasting B section, a final A′. The selector repeats specific phrase identifiers at the A slots (phrase reuse is a feature, not a bug) while the B section is drawn from a disjoint candidate pool under pre-dominant / dominant functional constraints.

Sample 2 — sentence_8 8 bars · 25 note events

A classical sentence: two-bar basic idea, its immediate repetition, a two-bar continuation with increased rhythmic density, and a two-bar half-cadence close. The constraint layer rejects candidates whose first pitch jumps more than five semitones from the previous phrase's last pitch, producing audibly stepwise voice leading.

Sample 3 — free_14 14 bars · 46 note events

No imposed form. A free chain of phrases selected only under the functional grammar T → {T, PD, D} and the voice-leading window. Useful for hearing what the raw selector prefers when no form-template biases the phrase-role distribution.

Selection traces

Each sample above has a corresponding entry in arcanet_log.txt listing, for every slot, the phrase identifier chosen, its chord, its functional role (T / PD / D), its density, and the top three candidates that were in contention. This is the artifact that makes PLCA-style composition inspectable: a musician can open the log, see that slot 4 was a coin-flip between two phrases, and re-run with a different seed or swap the candidates by hand.
